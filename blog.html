The Bewildering Experience of PC Building

Published by Joe Doe on 20 Apr 2017

I got my first IBM-compatible PC thanks to my generous uncle back in 1992. I was just an incipient teenager at the time, but I had some coding under my belt already thanks to the Commodore 64 that my dad let me co-opt a few years prior. Words cannot express the excitement I had at getting that 386SX with its reasonably peppy 25MHz Turbo processor, 2MB of RAM, and as I recall, a 40MB hard drive. Yes, I didn’t type those wrong — all Mega, no Giga.
